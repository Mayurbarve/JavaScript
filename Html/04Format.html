Physics(Text formats)

Physical science is a broad term encompassing the various branches of natural science that study non-living systems.

Physical science comprises four general areas: physics, astronomy, chemistry, and the Earth sciences.

Physics is a fundamental science because other natural sciences deal with systems that obey physics laws.

The physical laws of energy, matter, and nature’s forces govern the interactions between particles, such as atoms and molecules.
